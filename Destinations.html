PLACES TO GO

  • LUCENA PEREZ PARK

    PEREZ PARK Perez Park is a popular location for important events such as Pasayahan, Lucena Day, and Quezon Day. Political gatherings and competitions such as Battle of the Bands are also held here on occasion. Because it is a public space, anyone can use it as long as they coordinate with the GSO (Government Service Office). It's a popular hangout for families who let their children play in the park's swings, seesaws, and slides, as well as teens who utilize the park's expansive grounds to practice for school-related activities like group dances and choral recitations.

  • ST. FERDINAND CATEDRAL

    Lucena City Cathedral of Saint Ferdinand The Cathedral of St. Ferdinand has proudly stood as Lucena City's most recognizable feature for nearly a century. Founded in May 1882 and rebuilt in November 1887, this stunning piece of architecture is the enduring physical representation of the local diocese, which can be traced back to its first parish priest, Father Mariano Granja. The Lucena Cathedral (Filipino: Katedral ng Lucena), canonically the Saint Ferdinand Cathedral Parish (Filipino: Parokyang Katedral ni San Fernando), is a Roman Catholic cathedral in the Philippines' city of Lucena.
    SM CITY LUCENA

    SM offers so many shops, salons, arcades, and other attractions for tourists to enjoy, including eateries. It is being built by SM Prime Holdings, the Philippines' largest mall operator. It is situated on Maharlika Highway, corner Dalahican Road, Brgy. Ibabang Dupay, Lucena City, Quezon It is the first SM Supermall in the province of Quezon. It has a gross floor space of 78,655 m2 (846,640 sq ft) and a land area of 8 hectares. The three-level supermall with a floor space of 72,056 square meters will add excitement to SM's shopping, leisure, and entertainment offerings.
  • MUNTING TALAO-TALAO BEACH RESORT

    Talao-Talao Beach is a public beach in the Philippine province of Quezon. Talao-Talao Beach Resort is located in Lucena. Open around the clock. Talao-Talao is easily accessible, being only 5 minutes from Lucena (Bayan) and 4-5 hours from Metro Manila. It's perfect for people on a tight budget because there's no admission fee and certain amenities are also given for free. You can take in the scenery and ride a boat to white sand. It's ideal for a low-budget outing.
